# AI, Automation & Computer Vision

We live in a world where machines can see. From cameras that track athletes on a football pitch to systems that detect defects on a factory floor, computer vision is quietly powering some of the most exciting technology of our time. This course puts that power in your hands.

You will learn how to teach a computer to see, understand, and act on what it sees - using nothing more than a laptop and a webcam. You will write Python code to capture and process live video, train AI models to detect players and track a ball in real time, build JavaScript dashboards that display your results live in a browser, and connect everything to a large language model so your system can explain what it sees in plain English.

This is not a passive course. You will collect real data at a football field, deploy your pipeline at a live sports event, and present a working product to a real audience on demo day. Every concept you learn in the classroom gets tested in the field.

By the end, you will not just understand how AI and computer vision work - you will have built something with them. Something that perceives the world, makes decisions, and takes action. That is the definition of automation, and it starts here.
